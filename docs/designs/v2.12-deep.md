# CEO Plan: v2.12 "Deep" — Go Deep on the Two Bets + Feature Freeze

Generated 2026-04-17.
Branch: main (future: v2.12) | Follows: v2.11 "Solid" (~mid-May 2026)

---

## Premise

**v2.10 made the code safe. v2.11 makes it reliable. v2.12 answers: what is OSChief actually FOR?**

An external audit read our release history and said the uncomfortable truth: "OSChief has the surface area of a product three years old and the polish of a beta." 20+ feature surfaces (Jira, Asana, Slack, Teams, Gmail, Obsidian, Apple Calendar, Google Calendar, custom vocabulary, speaker diarization, sidebar folders, dark mode, 5 STT engines, memory graph, coaching, weekly digest, agent API, vault/keychain) for a solo-dev early-stage product. Every one is a maintenance tax. None are yet a reason someone recommends OSChief to a colleague.

The critic asked the right question: **which 2-3 features actually drive retention?**

v2.11's Stream 5 (PostHog analytics) gives us real data for the first time. v2.12 is where we act on it — double down on the 2 that drive retention, freeze the rest.

The gut answer, before data:
1. **Coaching with role-aware frameworks + cross-meeting personalization** — the provocative-question angle is unusual. Most meeting tools stop at transcription + action items.
2. **Meeting prep briefs** — "5 min before your 1:1 with Sarah, here's what you owe her, here are 3 talking points based on your history." This is the "tell a friend" moment that's been sitting in TODOS P2 since v2.7.

Everything else is parity. The v2.12 move is: **make 1 and 2 magical; freeze the rest.**

---

## Non-goals (explicit)

- **No new integrations.** No Notion, no Linear, no Monday, no HubSpot. If it's an external SaaS we don't already support, the answer is no for v2.12.
- **No new STT engines.** v2.11 ships the 4-5 we have (native whisper.cpp + Qwen3-ASR + Whisper variants + optional Parakeet). No Ai4Bharat IndicConformer unless v2.11's Indian-English eval shows WER > 15% after Stream 4 lands.
- **No new output formats.** No slides export, no Notion sync, no Roam, no Bear. Obsidian + markdown + DOCX + PDF is enough.
- **No new summary templates beyond the 7 built-ins.** Custom templates already exist.
- **No infra rewrites.** No switch from Electron, no migration off SQLite, no server-side anything.
- **No v3.0 agentic work.** That's its own release. Don't leak forward.

---

## Vision

After v2.12, a user who records one meeting should have a moment within 7 days where they say "this is genuinely useful, I'm going to tell [colleague]." That moment is either:

- **Coaching:** "OSChief just asked me a question about my career that my manager should have asked 6 months ago."
- **Prep brief:** "I walked into a meeting knowing exactly what I owed this person and what to ask. I didn't have to scramble through notes for 10 minutes beforehand."

If neither moment happens consistently for users by end of v2.12, OSChief hasn't found its wedge and we course-correct in v2.13.

## Scope (four streams, ~17-20 days)

**Gated on v2.11 Stream 5 shipping + PostHog data AND a user-interview sprint.** We need 4 weeks of PostHog data post-v2.11 release before starting v2.12, AND qualitative evidence from real users about what they value. Usage counts tell you WHAT users do. Interviews tell you WHY. Both matter. Rough calendar: v2.11 ships mid-May, v2.12 starts mid-June after 4 weeks of data + 3-4 day interview sprint.

### Stream 0: Evidence-first — user interview sprint — ~3-4 days

**Why this exists:** The coaching + prep-brief bets came from an external critic's framing, not from user evidence. Committing 14 days of build without user-confirmed demand is how products get built for critics instead of users. This stream de-risks that.

**Target interviewees (5-10 total):**
- 3-5 active users (recorded ≥5 meetings in the last 30 days per PostHog)
- 2-3 lapsed users (recorded 1-3 meetings, then stopped)
- 1-2 power users (top 10% by meeting count)

**30-minute semi-structured interviews. Questions:**
1. Walk me through the last time you used OSChief. What happened before, during, after?
2. What made you download it? What made you keep using it (or stop)?
3. If OSChief disappeared tomorrow, what would you use instead? What would you miss most?
4. Show me your last 3 meetings in OSChief. Which tabs did you click? Which did you ignore?
5. What's the 1 thing OSChief should do that it doesn't do well today?
6. Would you recommend it to a colleague? To whom? Why / why not?
7. **[Hypothesis probe]** Would a "5 min before your meeting, here's what to prep" notification be useful? Walk me through when you'd actually use it.
8. **[Hypothesis probe]** Would weekly coaching insights ("here's a pattern I noticed across your meetings") be valuable? What would make them useful vs annoying?

**Synthesis deliverable (`v2.12-interview-synthesis.md`):**
- Demand signal per hypothesis (strong / mixed / weak) with verbatim quotes
- Surprising wedges mentioned by multiple users that aren't in the current plan
- Top 3 pain points ranked by how often they came up
- GO / PIVOT / KILL recommendation for coaching depth
- GO / PIVOT / KILL recommendation for prep briefs
- If PIVOT: concrete scope adjustment proposal

**Decision gate after Stream 0:**
- **GO on both:** Proceed with Streams 1-3 as specified. No scope change.
- **GO on one, PIVOT on other:** Cut the pivoted bet from v2.12, reinvest days in the strong bet. Document the pivot in the CEO plan.
- **KILL both:** Stop. Do NOT build coaching + prep. Use the remaining v2.12 days for the wedge users actually asked for. This is the "we're building the wrong thing" escape hatch. Better to find out in Day 4 than Day 16.
- **Mixed/ambiguous signal:** Extend interviews by 3-5 users to resolve. Don't fake decisiveness on weak data.

**Ship gate for Stream 0:**
- Interview synthesis doc committed to repo (sanitized — no PII, rough themes only if users prefer).
- Explicit written decision on each hypothesis (GO/PIVOT/KILL) with evidence citations.
- If PIVOT or KILL: updated Streams 1-3 reflecting the change, reviewed by user before execution.

**Effort reality check:** 3-4 days is ~5-10 user interviews + 1 day synthesis. User recruitment via email to existing download list (PostHog can enumerate install_ids, map to email via whatever channel they installed through). If recruitment takes longer than 2 days, cut target to 5 interviews and ship the synthesis with that sample.

### Original scope (gated on Stream 0 decision)

### Stream 1: Coaching depth — cross-meeting personalization — ~6 days

**What v2.10 shipped (baseline):** Role-aware coaching KB with 10 personas, per-meeting insights, conversation analysis tab, habit tags.

**What's missing to make it "magical":**
1. **Cross-meeting memory.** Today each meeting's coaching is standalone. It doesn't know you've been avoiding the raise conversation for 3 weeks, or that your filler-word habit spiked after your team grew.
2. **Proactive surfacing.** Insights live in the Coaching tab. Most users never click it. The useful ones should bubble up — as a weekly digest item, as a tray-level notification, as a card on the Today page.
3. **Actionable specificity.** "You interrupted 8 times" is a stat. "You interrupted Sarah 3 of the 4 times she brought up the architecture concern" is a coaching moment.

| # | Item | Effort | Why |
|---|------|--------|-----|
| C1 | Cross-meeting coaching aggregator — weekly rollup of filler-word trends, talk ratio drift, topic-avoidance patterns, frequently-interrupted-by-you people. Stored as `coaching_trends` table scoped to 30 days rolling. | M | Required infrastructure for C2-C4. |
| C2 | **Proactive coaching card on Today page** — top 1-2 insights from the week that are worth acting on. Not a dashboard, not a list — a single actionable observation. "You interrupted Sarah 3 times this week when she raised architecture concerns. Next time she speaks up, try waiting 5 seconds." | M | This is where coaching stops being optional. |
| C3 | **Career-strategy provocations** (v2.8 direction, deeper) — monthly-cadence LLM pass over the last 30 days of transcripts + commitments + decisions. Generates 1-3 provocative questions the user isn't asking themselves. "You've had 8 meetings about the Q3 roadmap but none with your skip-level. Is that deliberate?" Delivered via weekly digest OR on-demand via Ask OSChief. | M | The "tell a friend" vector for coaching. |
| C4 | Topic-avoidance detection — scan transcript series for topics that appear briefly then disappear before resolution. Surface as "Unresolved: Q2 budget mentioned in 3 meetings, never closed." | S | Complements C3 — surfaces the uncomfortable stuff. |
| C5 | Conversation-insight eval suite — 20 transcript fixtures with hand-labeled expected insights, regression gated in CI. Prevents the title-saga pattern (ship → user hits issue → patch → patch → patch). | S | Critic's "test the prompt against 50 failure cases before shipping" feedback applied. |
| C6 | Settings → Coaching → depth slider (off / weekly-only / daily) — respects the user's tolerance for self-improvement prompts. Default = weekly. | XS | Some users want daily, most don't. |

### Stream 2: Meeting prep briefs — ship the "killer feature" — ~4 days

**Status:** Sitting in TODOS as P2 since ~v2.7. Memory layer (people, commitments, decisions, projects) is ready. Calendar integration is ready. The remaining work is the brief itself + the notification moment.

| # | Item | Effort | Why |
|---|------|--------|-----|
| P1 | `memory/prep-brief.ts` — takes calendar event (attendees + title + time), queries people table + note_people + commitments + decisions + projects, assembles context. LLM call generates 3-5 line brief. Ship as an IPC channel consumed by the tray + notification + in-app card. | M | Infrastructure. Has been sitting there. Do it. |
| P2 | **5-min-before notification** — macOS notification with title + body. Click opens in-app prep card. "Meeting with Sarah Chen in 5 min — last discussed Q3 budget, you owe her the revised forecast, ask about the hiring freeze status." | S | The "tell a friend" moment. |
| P3 | In-app prep card — shown at top of Today page when a meeting is within 30 min. Shows full brief + "open note" button that starts recording with context pre-loaded. | S | Discoverability path that doesn't require notification permission. |
| P4 | Prep-brief eval — 10 calendar events with hand-curated expected brief content, scored by a judge model for helpfulness + accuracy. Regression gated in CI. | S | Prevent silent quality degradation. |
| P5 | "New prep brief" Ask OSChief slash prompt — `/prep Sarah` or `/prep tomorrow's meeting` — on-demand brief outside the 5-min window. | XS | Power user flow. |
| P6 | Measure in Stream-5 analytics — `prep_brief_notification_clicked`, `prep_brief_in_app_viewed`, `meeting_started_from_prep_card`. These are the retention signal for this feature. | XS | Know if it's actually driving retention. |

## Retention decision gate (entry criterion)

Before Stream 1 starts, make the call on what PostHog data says:

1. **Coaching metric:** % of users who recorded a meeting AND viewed the Coaching tab within 7 days. Target: > 40%. If < 20%, Stream 1 scope shifts to "make coaching discoverable before making it deeper."
2. **Prep brief signal:** This feature doesn't exist yet, so we can't measure it directly. But proxy: % of users who opened the calendar page within 24h of a recorded meeting. Target: > 30%. If < 15%, the prep-brief bet might not have pull.
3. **Feature usage distribution:** Top 3 features by usage, bottom 5 by usage. The bottom 5 list is the v2.13 kill candidates.

**If coaching usage < 20% AND calendar usage < 15%**: v2.12 pivots. We stop investing in coaching + prep and do a week of user interviews to find the actual wedge before building anything else.

**If data is ambiguous (20-40% range for coaching)**: proceed with Stream 1 + 2 as planned, but halve the scope of C3 (career-strategy provocations — the riskiest item) and reinvest in C2 (proactive card) to make the existing coaching more discoverable.

## Resolved decisions (pre-review)

1. **Two bets, not three.** Original temptation was coaching + prep + "improve memory graph viz." Dropped the third — memory graph is already usable, doesn't need attention in v2.12.
2. **Feature freeze is documented, not enforced by tooling.** A CI check that blocks PRs touching `FROZEN-FEATURES.md` paths is tempting but adds friction for real bug fixes. Doc + discipline.
3. **Single shipping event, not two.** v2.11 had two (silent auto-install + user-prompted) because reliability changes benefit from soak time. v2.12 is feature-forward — ship one moment that matches the "go deep" narrative.
4. **Retention gate is PostHog-only, not user interviews.** If the data is ambiguous, THEN we do interviews. Starting with interviews adds weeks before v2.12 begins.
5. **Prep-brief notification uses macOS Notification API, not a custom window.** Electron's Notification class is mature; custom window = more failure modes. Same pattern as existing meeting-detected notification.
6. **Coaching depth slider default = weekly, not daily.** Daily feels like harassment for most users. Users who want more can opt in.

## The uncomfortable acknowledgement

v2.10 + v2.11 + v2.12 is a 3-release arc that adds almost no new user-facing surface area. That's deliberate. The first 9 releases went wide (v2.1 → v2.9). The next 3 go deep. If v2.12 ships and coaching + prep don't drive measurable retention improvement in the 4 weeks after, the product hasn't found its wedge and v2.13 becomes a pivot — user interviews, hard cuts, possibly renaming the category.

The external critic's framing is correct: **this is a wedge test.** v2.12 is us answering "is OSChief a coaching tool that happens to record meetings, or is it a meeting recorder that happens to coach?" The first is a real product. The second is parity.
